Here's an example of color with RGB values.
Here you set each value of Red, Green, Blue from 0-255.
All 0 is Black and all 255 is White.

And another with HEX values.
Here you set rr, gg, and bb to values from 00-ff.
Thusly, #ff0000 is the same as 255, 00, 00 with RGB.

Also one with HSL values!
Now we're getting fancy with hue, saturation, and lightness.
Hue (color shade) is a color wheel from 0-360. 0 is Red, 120 is Green. 240 is Blue.
Saturation (intensity of color) is a percentage value with 0% being a shade of grey and 100% being full color.
Lightness (how much light you provide) is also a percentage value with 0% being Black, 50% being neither, and 100% being White.

Even one with RGBA values for "transparency"!

Same goes for this example with HSLA values.

Both of the above are like RGB/HSL but with an additional Alpha.
At 0.0 it's fully transparent and at 1.0 it's not transparent at all.
